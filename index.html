Selected Projects

  • Learning to Transfer via Modelling Multi-level Task Dependency
    [pdf]
    Advisor: Prof. Yizhou Sun (UCLA)
    We developed an attention-based framework to capture both general and data-specific (i.e. fine-grained) tasks dependency structures. We utilized the multi-level task dependency structures to decide what knowledge should be transferred, and the aligned hyper-graph to decide how knowledge should be transferred.
  • Chemical Property Prediction via Graph Knowledge Transfer
    [slides] [poster]
    Advisor: Prof. Yizhou Sun (UCLA)
    I proposed to transfer knowledge across tasks to improve the label efficiency. The key point here is to leverage the graph structure and use the aligned hyper-graph to decide how knowledge should be transferred. This project was selected for the final presentation of UCLA-CSST program, and I also received the Outstanding Research and Presentation Skills Award (Top 10%).
  • Development of AI Front-view Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS)
    [video]
    Advisor: Dr. Laiye Bi (Leadmove Technology (Shanghai) Co., Ltd.)
    I developed a camera calibration system based on optical flow and Newton’s method, which improved the accuracy and efficiency of online camera calibration. I also developed an image post-processing system, which integrated distortion correction, perspective mapping, position detection, and speed measurement.
  • GPU Accelerated Mitosis Cell Detection
    [code] [slides]
    Advisor: Prof. Steven Lumetta (UIUC)
    We developed a convolutional neural network to detect mitosis cells and optimized the CUDA implementation, which accelerated the inference speed by 181%.
  • Linux System Design
    [code]
    Advisor: Prof. Zbigniew Kalbarczyk and Prof. Jian Huang (UIUC)
    We developed the core of an operating system from scratch. We implemented several advanced functionalities, such as the graphical terminal, Chinese input method editor, and drivers for sound and mouse. In the end, we ranked No. 1 among 54 groups in the design competition.
